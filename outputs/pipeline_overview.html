4
Three Econometric Models
Model 1 — Baseline
Pooled Logit
All firm-months pooled together. No industry or time adjustment. Ohlson (1980) style.

Input: features → Output: P(deterioration)
statsmodels.Logit(y, X).fit()
Model 2 — Main
Fixed-Effects Logit
Adds industry dummies (SIC) + year dummies. Controls for "tech vs energy" and "2020 vs 2015." Clustered SEs by month.

Why better: compares apples to apples
Model 3 — Academic
Hazard Logit
Shumway (2001) survival approach. Firms exit after first event. Models "given survival until now, what's the risk this month?"

Why needed: theoretically correct for first-time events

Evaluation & Metrics
5 Key Metrics
How good are the predictions?
AUROC — Can it rank risky above safe? (higher = better)
AUPRC — Does it find the rare bad events? (critical for imbalanced data)
Brier Score — Are probabilities honest? (lower = better)
Top-10% Capture — Watchlist effectiveness (practical metric)
Lead Time — How many months early does it warn? (earlier = better)

Calibration & Explainability
Coefficients
Odds Ratios + Marginal Effects
"1-unit increase in leverage multiplies odds of trouble by 10.5x"
Makes model decisions transparent and explainable to examiners
Calibration
Reliability Curves + Brier
Plot predicted probability vs actual event rate. Perfect model = diagonal line. Shows if model is overconfident or underconfident.
Monitoring
Risk-Decile Chart
Divide firms into 10 buckets by predicted risk. Top decile should have dramatically higher event rate → proves watchlist works.

7
Robustness Checks
Check 1
Threshold Sensitivity
Re-run with -30%, -40%, -50% drawdown thresholds for Label A. Results should be directionally consistent.
Check 2
Rolling Windows
Train on [start→T], test on T+1, repeat yearly. Shows model is stable across different time periods, not just one lucky split.
Check 3 (optional)
Utility Exclusion
Remove utility companies (regulated balance sheets) and re-run. Checks if results hold without a specific sector.
